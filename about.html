HISTORY OF TUP VISAYAS

VISAYAS TECHNICIAN INSTITUTE (1977–1985)

The Technological University of the Philippines Visayas (TUP Visayas) originated as the Visayas Technician Institute (VTI) in 1977. Established in Talisay City, Negros Occidental, VTI was one of three prototype technician institutes initiated by the Philippine government under the World Bank-funded Credit Agreement 349 PH. These institutes aimed to enhance technical education and develop middle-level manpower across the country. VTI commenced its operations by offering specialized technician programs to cater to the growing industrial and technological needs of the Visayas region.



INTEGRATION INTO THE TUP SYSTEM (1978–1985)

In 1978, Presidential Letter of Instruction No. 79 established the National Polytechnic System, designating the Technological University of the Philippines (TUP) as its apex institution. This directive integrated various state-supported technical institutions, including VTI, into the TUP system. Consequently, VTI came under the administration and supervision of TUP, aligning its programs and operations with the university's standards and objectives. This integration aimed to unify and strengthen technical education across the nation.



RENAMING TO TUP VISAYAS (1985–PRESENT)

In 1985, pursuant to TUP Implementing Order No. 85-15, the Visayas Technician Institute was officially renamed the Technological University of the Philippines Visayas (TUP Visayas). This change marked its full integration into the TUP system as a satellite campus. Situated in Talisay City, Negros Occidental, TUP Visayas expanded its reach by establishing extension campuses in Sagay City and Cadiz City. The campus broadened its academic offerings to include three-year technology courses, bachelor's degrees, and master's degrees in various fields of engineering and technology.



CONTRIBUTIONS AND RECOGNITION

Over the years, TUP Visayas has been recognized as a leading institution in engineering and technological education in the Philippines. It has consistently produced topnotchers in the Professional Licensure Examinations conducted by the Professional Regulation Commission (PRC). The campus's commitment to excellence has earned it national and international accolades, including recognition as a Center of Excellence by the ASEAN Economic Ministers - Ministry of International Trade and Industry, Economic and Industrial Committee (AIMEICC) Working Group on Human Resource Development. TUP Visayas continues to play a pivotal role in advancing technical education and contributing to regional and national development.
